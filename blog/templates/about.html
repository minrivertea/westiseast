My name is Chris West, and I'm a British photographer and web project manager living in Fuzhou, China. This blog is about my two of my favourite things - black and white photography and life for expats in China.

The cameras I use

I don't have a massive camera or lens collection. Mostly because I don't have enough money for one! However, I've chosen wisely and got myself a Nikon D300 with the 18-200mm VR lens, and a Nikkor 50mm 1.8F portrait lens. Recently, I also bought a Hasselblad 500C/M, which is a medium format (6x6) film camera. It rocks.

My day job

I run a Chinese tea shop called The Min River Tea Farm - it's an online shop where you can buy great quality Chinese teas in the UK. If you like tea, or want something different for your daily brew, check it out!

Why am I in China?

I first got interested in China and Chinese culture after studying martial arts with the Fujian White Crane kung fu club. I travelled to China with a martial arts research group about 5 times, taking over 30,000 photographs and hours of videos of authentic, traditional martial arts. After a few years, I decided to move to China, learn the language and extend my knowledge of this beautiful country.
